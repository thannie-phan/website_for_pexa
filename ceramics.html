About

Thannie Phan (b. Kien Giang, Vietnam) is the artist behind g·ªëm maker, a ceramic practice shaped by her experiences in Sai Gon, Singapore, Auckland, Gadigal Country (Sydney), and Naarm (Melbourne), where she currently resides.

g·ªëm maker is her ceramic practice, shaped by the stories she writes about where she is, where she was, the people she inhabited these spaces with, and the imprints they leave behind.

The vessels are made after, serving as physical interpretations of these narratives. Each piece is shaped through the quiet rigour of hand-building processes, using very fine coils pinched into form, then coloured using layers of slip and oxide that's brushed or stamped onto the surface. The edges remain raw, the palette restrained, and the weight calibrated to presence rather than function.
